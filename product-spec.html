Executive Summary

What SlashCard is, who it's for, and why it exists. A one-page brief.

Product in one line

SlashCard is a dark-themed, dark-horse alternative to generic crypto card aggregators like todey.xyz. Instead of a static comparison table, it's a living intelligence platform — featuring real user reviews, live problem reports, curated promo/fee intel, and a social verification layer.

The problem we solve

Crypto card users today rely on scattered sources to make decisions:

  • Twitter/X — noisy, unstructured, biased
  • Telegram groups — buried conversations, per-card silos
  • Aggregator sites (todey.xyz, cryptonomist) — one-time visit, no community signal, no real user experience
  • Official card websites — marketing fluff, no incident data

There is no canonical destination for the question: "What's happening with crypto card X right now?"

Core MVP features

🎴 Card Directory

15 pre-listed crypto cards with rendered visuals, ratings, and metadata. Filterable by type (CEX / DeFi / Prepaid / Debit).

⭐️ User Reviews

1-5 star rating + free-text review + optional screenshot. No auth required, 10-char minimum.

/ Slash Reports

Quick incident reports (frozen funds, delays, KYC issues). Powers the Live Status indicator.

📡 Intelligence Feed

Ops-curated intel: promos, fee changes, feature updates, tips. With community confirm/deny.

🚦 Live Status Indicator

Green / Yellow / Red status per card, driven by 7-day rolling slash count.

📤 Shareable Status Card

Auto-generate a PNG status card post-submission. One-click share to X.

Target user

Crypto-native consumers holding 1-3 crypto cards, actively spending USDC/USDT, primarily in APAC and EU regions. Age 22-40. Active on Crypto Twitter.

Core success metric

North Star

When someone asks "Is card X worth getting?" or "Is card X having issues?" — SlashCard is the link that gets sent in reply.

Product Vision & Thesis

Our positioning against competitors and the core product bet.

The frequency thesis

There's a spectrum between one-time aggregator and daily habit app. Most crypto card users don't need a daily habit. But they need more than a one-time comparison site.

Revisit frequency spectrum
  One-time                           Weekly                          Daily
  │                                     │                               │
  todey.xyz ─────────────── SlashCard ────────────── Twitter/X
  (open once to pick)       (return for events +                 (daily scroll)
                             promos + before top-up)

Why users return to SlashCard

  1. Before a top-up — Check current fees, active promos, system status. Weekly, reflexive check.
  2. When they see card news on X — Click a SlashCard link to get the full picture. Event-driven.
  3. When they're burned — File a slash report to warn others. Emotion-driven, high virality.
  4. When a friend asks — Send a link instead of typing a long answer. Offloads information work.

User Personas

Three primary personas drive our design decisions.

P1

The Card Shopper

Profile: About to get their first crypto card. Has ETH/USDC, wants to spend it IRL.

Goal: Pick the right card without getting burned.

Pain: Too many options, can't trust card issuer marketing, wants real experiences.

Visits: 2-3 times during research phase. Then rarely.

P2

The Multi-Card Optimizer

Profile: Holds 2-3 crypto cards. Uses them interchangeably based on context.

Goal: Maximize value per transaction. Avoid fees, catch promos.

Pain: Card issuers silently change fees. Promos buried in TG.

Visits: Weekly before top-ups. Core revisit persona.

P3

The Burned User

Profile: Just had a bad experience. Card frozen, funds stuck, support AWOL.

Goal: Vent, warn others, find out if they're alone.

Pain: Angry. Wants immediate action.

Visits: Once during incident. High-virality emission.

Persona priority in V1

P2 (Multi-Card Optimizer) is our primary retention target. P3 (Burned User) is our primary acquisition driver via shareable status cards. P1 (Card Shopper) is the secondary acquisition target (via SEO + organic discovery).

Simplification rationale

Why we dropped the 6-dimension rating

Original spec had 6 separate 1-5 ratings (top-up speed, fees, chains, freeze risk, support, KYC). For a cold-start product with zero reviews, asking users to fill out 6 sliders is a conversion killer. MVP uses 1 overall rating. Multi-dimension ratings return in V2 when we have volume.

Validation & error handling

  • Errors are shown inline in a red callout above the submit button
  • One error at a time (first-failure wins), not a list
  • Error copy is direct: "Please select a rating" — not "A valid rating is required"
  • No per-field inline errors (too noisy for a short form)

Success state

On submit: full-page success view → auto-redirect to card detail after 1.5s.

Confirm / Deny mechanism

The single most important UX decision in V1

Confirm/Deny is the only user-contributed data for intel items in V1. Because it's a single click, it converts passive readers into participants at a ratio of roughly 100:1 vs. any UGC submission.

Button style
[✓ N] green outline · [✗ N] red outline. Compact (8×24px padding). Tabular-nums.
One-time vote
After voting, both buttons disabled. Selected button filled, other faded (opacity 0.4).
Persistence
V1: In-memory only (resets on reload). V2: localStorage + server-side dedup.
Moderation
If denies > confirms + 3, flag for ops review. Item auto-hidden if denies > 10. [V1.1]

Feed filtering

Tab control at top of feed: All · Intel · Reports

  • All: intel + slash + reviews, chronological
  • Intel: ops-posted intel only (promo/fee/feature/tip)
  • Reports: slash reports only

Feed ordering

V1: Strict chronological (newest first).

V2 consideration: Weighted by confirms - denies (quality signal). Pin high-priority items (new promos, red-status card alerts) for 24h.

Content Supply Model

Who creates what. The critical distinction between V1 and competitors.

The supply split

OPS / ADMIN

Team-curated content

  • 🎁 Promo intel
  • 📊 Fee changes
  • 🆕 Feature updates
  • 💡 Tips

Source: Monitor TG groups, X accounts, official announcements. Seeded manually via Supabase dashboard in V1 (no admin UI needed).

USERS

User-contributed content

  • ⭐️ Reviews (full submission)
  • / Slash reports (full submission)
  • ✓/✗ Confirm/Deny (one-click)

Motivation: Reviews = reputation-free catharsis. Reports = anger. Confirms = near-zero cost participation.

Why we don't accept user-submitted intel in V1

Cold-start reality check

We analyzed the motivation for each content type. Positive intel (promos, tips) requires either reputation, karma, or community belonging to incentivize creation. V1 has none of these. Asking anonymous users to submit intel = empty intel feed.

Instead: Team owns supply in V1. Users own validation (confirm/deny). When user volume justifies it (~1000 DAU), unlock user-submitted intel with optional moderation.

Ops playbook (for internal team)

  1. Daily: Scan X + TG groups for card news. Post as intel items with source link.
  2. Weekly: Publish at least 1 tip. Aim for 4-8 promo items if deals exist.
  3. Monthly: Audit fee change list across cards. Compare to official rate cards.
  4. Event-driven: When a card has incident spike, write a summary intel item contextualizing the activity.

Status Indicator Logic

How we decide green / yellow / red.

The rule

  GREEN  (Normal)      recent_slash_count ≤ 5
  YELLOW (Issues)      5 < recent_slash_count ≤ 15
  RED    (Critical)    recent_slash_count > 15

  recent_slash_count = slash_reports WHERE created_at > now() - 7 days

Why a sliding 7-day window

Fixed thresholds over "all time" would cause status to become permanent. Sliding window means status self-heals as old reports age out. If a card had a spike 2 months ago but is fine now, it returns to green.

Why absolute thresholds (not ratios)

Ratio-based thresholds (e.g., "reports / users") require knowing user base per card — we don't. Absolute thresholds are simple and calibrated against expected volume. Once we have traffic data, tune thresholds per card popularity.

Visual treatment

GREEN
Badge "Normal". Pulsing dot. No glow on cards. Calm, unobtrusive.
YELLOW
Badge "Issues (N)". Pulsing amber dot. Subtle yellow glow on detail page. Pulsing alert banner on homepage.

Anti-gaming considerations

Risk: Spam / competitor attacks

In V1, submission is open and anonymous. A competitor could submit 20 slash reports to turn a rival red.

Mitigation (V1.1): Rate limit by IP + browser fingerprint (1 report per card per 24h). Require confirm/deny votes to count toward status calc (low-confirm reports get weighted less).

Copy & Voice

Tone guidelines and microcopy conventions.

Voice principles

✓ DO

  • Direct. "Please select a rating" not "A valid rating must be selected"
  • Neutral on cards. Don't root for or against any card.
  • Factual on problems. "3 reports this week" not "disaster unfolding"
  • Lowercase except proper nouns.
  • Use em-dashes (—) for pause, not colons.

✕ DON'T

  • Preach. (Dropped "No shills. No sponsors.")
  • Marketing fluff. No "revolutionary" "game-changing".
  • Emojis in body copy. Only in specific UI slots (intel type icons).
  • Apologize. "Report submitted" not "Sorry for the trouble!"
  • Exclamation marks. Ever.

How SlashCard grows itself, no paid acquisition.

Viral coefficient estimation

Every slash submission → ~25% click "Share to X" (based on analogous fintech incident apps) → average tweet reach ~300 followers → ~2% CTR from tweet to SlashCard → ~0.15 new visits per submission.

At that rate, we need an amplification mechanism beyond just slash reports. Planned additions:

  • Auto-tweet from ops: When a card hits RED status, ops manually tweets the status card image (no UI change needed, just a playbook)
  • Weekly digest: Email subscribers get "This week in crypto cards" — high-signal, low-effort retention
  • Embeddable status widget: Card issuers / bloggers can embed "current status of X card" badge → backlinks (V2)

Open Questions & V2 Roadmap

Things to discuss and build next.

Open design questions

  1. Sort "Trending" semantics: Should it surface cards with most reports (current) or most recent activity regardless of sign? I.e., a card with lots of new promos should also "trend."
  2. Alert banner selection: Currently shows first red/yellow. Should it be the newest incident? The highest count? User-configurable?
  3. Review form weight: Is 10-char min the right bar? Too high kills conversion. Too low invites spam. Monitor spam rate in V1.1.
  4. Intel ordering in feed: Pure chronological vs. weighted by confirms? Pinning mechanism needed?
  5. CTA priority on detail page: Review vs. Slash — currently Review is primary (indigo). Is that right for a card with active issues? Context-switch to Slash as primary when status is red?

V1.1 (weeks 1-2 post-launch)

  • Supabase backend (replace mock data)
  • Rate limiting on submissions (1 per IP per card per 24h)
  • Replace native alert() with toast component
  • Add empty states for filtered grid
  • Add prefers-reduced-motion support
  • Card logo auto-fetch on card add (Clearbit Logo API)

V2 (1-3 months)

  • Email/TG bot "Watch a card" — push notifications for fee changes, status changes, new promos
  • Admin dashboard for ops intel posting (replace Supabase dashboard)
  • Review search and filter
  • Card comparison view ("Rain vs RedotPay")
  • Multi-dimension rating (return of the 6-dimension rating from original spec)
  • Upvote/downvote reviews for quality signal
  • Chinese localization (simplified Chinese primary)

V3 speculation

  • Mobile app

Design decisions log

DecisionReasoning
Dark theme native (not light with dark toggle)Crypto audience skews dark. Cleaner brand identity. One fewer mode to maintain.
Rendered CC visuals (not logos)todey.xyz uses card product shots (expensive to license). Our CSS-rendered cards avoid legal risk and look distinctive.
Anonymous submission (no auth)Lower friction > higher quality. Quality problems solved by confirm/deny mechanism instead of auth.
Ops-only intel creationCold-start won't generate user intel. Team acts as editorial.
Single rating (not 6-dimension)Conversion over taxonomy. Multi-dimension returns in V2 with volume.
No auto-redirect after slashSlash submission IS the growth event. Dedicated share view captures that value.
No user intel submissionWeak motivation without reputation. V2 feature.
Slash name kept (not "Report")"Slash" is distinctive, product-defining, and the / icon IS the brand.
